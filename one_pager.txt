AIiTAPP — Technical Overview
Delegation Scoring for AI Research Agents
April 2026 | Confidential | AyenaSelf


THE PROBLEM
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
AI research agents report confidence in their recommendations — not the quality
of the evidence behind them. A pattern surfaced from stale, contradicted, or
poorly-sourced data can carry an 8/10 confidence score. The researcher sees
the confidence. Not the evidence quality. The wrong branch is taken.

McKinsey documented 44% variability in professional judgment on identical
cases — caused not by incompetence, but by inconsistency in evidence quality
at the moment of decision. Kahneman called this noise. AIiTAPP measures it.


WHAT AIiTAPP DOES
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
AIiTAPP intercepts a proposed agent action BEFORE it reaches the researcher.
It scores the evidence behind the action across six dimensions and assigns
an authority state. The researcher receives an evidence packet — not a
raw recommendation.

The system does not replace researcher judgment.
It scores the evidentiary basis of the AI's recommendation at the moment
it is made, before a human acts on it.


THE DELEGATION SCORE
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
S = (C × 0.15) + (P × 0.30) + (T × 0.20) + (R × 0.25) + (N × 0.07) + (F × 0.03)

  C  Confidence       Agent-reported certainty (0-10)
  P  Provenance       Evidence traceability and source quality (0-10)  ← elevated in medical
  T  Trust History    Agent calibration from prior outcomes (0-10)
  R  Risk             Inverted from risk class: critical=1.0 / high=3.0  ← elevated in medical
  N  Contradiction    Inverted from conflict count: 0 conflicts=9.0 / 1=6.0
  F  Freshness        Age of oldest evidence: <1h=9.0 / <24h=7.0 / >720h=1.0

Score range: 0.0 – 10.0. Computed deterministically. Explainable at every dimension.


THE FOUR AUTHORITY STATES
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  GREEN  ≥ 7.5   Autonomous — recommendation passes to researcher
  YELLOW 5.5–7.4 Draft only — quick human review before researcher sees it
  ORANGE 3.5–5.4 PAUSE — evidence packet generated — researcher sees gap analysis
  RED    < 3.5   ABSTAIN — mandatory escalation — agent cannot proceed


THE HUMAN INSERTION PACKET
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
When a recommendation scores below GREEN, the system generates a structured
three-item evidence packet for the reviewer:

  1. Highest-scoring dimension — why the recommendation has partial support
  2. Lowest-scoring dimension — the primary evidence weakness
  3. Flagged dimension — if Contradiction or Freshness scores below 4.0

Example output (ORANGE state):
  ✅ Strongest: Confidence (8.2/10) — Agent certainty is high
  ⚠️  Weakest:  Freshness (1.0/10) — Oldest source is 47 days old
  🚩 Flagged:  Contradiction detected against Dataset 4 (not in query)
  → Recommended: Verify Dataset 3 freshness. Resolve contradiction before acting.


OUTCOME-BASED RECALIBRATION
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Every scored action, human decision, and eventual outcome is logged.
The system computes a rolling calibration score for each agent:

  Correct=10.0 / Mostly correct=7.0 / Incorrect=3.0 / Harmful=0.0

This calibration score becomes the Trust History dimension in future scoring
for the same agent — creating a closed feedback loop. Agents that make
accurate recommendations earn higher authority. Agents that surface false
patterns lose it. The system gets more accurate with use.


AGENT-TO-AGENT TRUST DISCOUNTING
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
In federated research pipelines where one agent's output feeds another,
AIiTAPP discounts Provenance quality based on the upstream agent's
calibration score. Low-reliability upstream sources cannot contaminate
downstream authority decisions.


INTELLECTUAL FOUNDATION
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
AIiTAPP applies Kahneman, Sibony & Sunstein's Noise Theory to machine agents.
The same agent, given the same task type, may have high-quality evidence in
one instance and low-quality evidence in another. The delegation score is a
noise-reduction mechanism — making authority decisions consistent, calibrated,
and traceable regardless of variability in evidence quality.
